    Main Attractions

    • Marina Bay Sands

      Marina Bay Sands is Singapore's most iconic integrated resort, featuring world-class entertainment facilities and luxury hotels. The three 55-story hotel towers are topped by the spectacular Sands SkyPark, offering 360-degree panoramic views. The resort includes a shopping mall, ArtScience Museum, theaters, and Singapore's largest casino.

      The most famous infinity pool is located 57 floors above ground, the world's largest elevated infinity pool. The pool edge seems to merge with the city skyline, making it one of the most popular Instagram spots. At night, visitors can enjoy spectacular city light shows and musical fountain performances. Marina Bay Sands has completely transformed Singapore's skyline, perfectly combining modern architecture with luxurious living.

    • Merlion Park

      The Merlion is Singapore's most famous national icon and tourism symbol. This 8.6-meter tall, 70-ton statue was designed by sculptor Lim Nang Seng, combining a lion's head with a fish's body. Located along Marina Bay, Merlion Park is a must-visit spot for tourists, offering magnificent panoramic views of the bay.

      According to legend, in the 14th century, a prince discovered this island while sailing and saw a mystical creature. His attendants told him it was a lion, so he named the island "Singapura" (Lion City). The water-spouting Merlion symbolizes Singapore's transformation from a fishing village to a modern metropolis. At night, the Merlion becomes even more mysteriously charming under the lights.

    • Sentosa Island

      Sentosa Island is Singapore's premier resort destination, covering approximately 5 square kilometers. The island boasts numerous world-class attractions including Universal Studios Singapore, S.E.A. Aquarium, and Adventure Cove Waterpark. White sandy beaches, tropical rainforests, and luxury resorts combine to create this dreamlike vacation paradise.

      "Sentosa" means "peace and tranquility" in Malay. The southernmost point of the island marks the southernmost tip of continental Asia. Visitors can reach the island via cable car or by walking across the Sentosa Boardwalk. The Resorts World Sentosa integrated resort offers diverse entertainment options, from thrilling rides to high-end shopping experiences, suitable for visitors of all ages.

    • Gardens by the Bay

      Gardens by the Bay is Singapore's newest landmark attraction, spanning 101 hectares across three waterfront gardens. The most striking features are the 18 "Supertrees" ranging from 25-50 meters tall. These vertical gardens provide shade during the day and transform into spectacular light show stages at night. Two massive conservatories - Flower Dome and Cloud Forest - showcase botanical wonders from around the world.

      The Flower Dome is the world's largest columnless glass greenhouse, recreating Mediterranean climates; the Cloud Forest simulates tropical mountain environments with a 35-meter indoor waterfall. Gardens by the Bay perfectly embodies Singapore's "City in a Garden" vision, skillfully blending nature with technology. The aerial walkway connects several Supertrees, allowing visitors to stroll 22 meters above ground while enjoying panoramic city views.
